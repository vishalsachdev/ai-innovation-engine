The Opportunity

Every top business school is scrambling to teach AI. Most are adding courses. A few are adding labs. None have built a self-sustaining engine where the act of teaching AI simultaneously generates research data, produces institutional infrastructure, creates a corporate talent pipeline, and feeds a startup accelerator.

Gies can be first — and we already have the pieces.

What We Propose

The AI Innovation Engine is a multi-sided platform where faculty, staff, corporate partners, and alumni post real-world AI challenges, and Gies students build solutions using shared open-source infrastructure. Every project adds to a compound skill library that makes the next project faster, generates annotated research data for studying human-AI collaboration, and feeds our Origin Ventures commercialization pipeline.

This is not a new program — it is an AI coordination layer on top of infrastructure that already exists: the Magelli Office (200+ client projects/year), Origin Ventures ($5M endowment, iVenture Accelerator), ISOI’s research networks, and our online programs (5,000+ students across 85 countries).

How It Works

Demand side: Faculty post course tool needs. Staff post operational automations. Corporate partners (Deloitte, Synchrony, Goldman Sachs, Bain — all currently in our DBC/CSP network) post talent-pipeline challenges with funding attached. Online MBA students bring challenges from their own employers.

Supply side: Student builder teams — IBC (260 consultants), Disruption Lab, AgentLab, EntreCorps, and individual students through BADM 301 (required for all juniors) — build solutions using shared technical infrastructure: an AI agent backend with university SSO, a skills framework for engineering process, an organizational simulation platform for teaching AI governance, and our existing Canvas MCP (90+ tools, open-source, adopted across 6 courses).

The flywheel: Every completed project deposits a reusable AI skill into a compound library. Next semester’s students start with a richer toolkit. Corporate sponsors see results and post more challenges. Research data flows to ISOI and faculty studying human-AI collaboration. Viable tools enter Origin Ventures’ iVenture Accelerator for commercialization. Alumni see innovation at Gies and engage as mentors and funders.

Scale

Gies has 9,000 active students (3,000 undergrad, 1,000 on-campus masters, 5,000+ online), 60,000+ alumni, and partnerships with 30+ corporations who are actively asking: “How do we build AI-ready talent?” The Engine gives them an answer — and a hiring pipeline.

Our online MBA students (average 11 years of work experience, spanning 85 countries) are a particularly powerful and untapped asset. Through a “Bring Your Own Challenge” model, they can apply AI skills directly to problems at their current employers — creating value for the student, the employer, and the skill library simultaneously.
