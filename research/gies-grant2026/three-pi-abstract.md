# Abstract

**Title:** The Gies AI Innovation Engine: Shared Research Infrastructure for AI-Driven Business Education

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Business Administration
**Co-Is:** Qingquan Zhang, Clinical Assistant Professor, Finance; Matias Carrasco Kind, Research Assistant Professor, Accountancy / Director, DSRS

**Funding Request:** Medium ($75,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Field study, archival, experimental

---

Every semester, thousands of Gies students use AI tools across business courses, yet this activity produces no shared research data and no reusable infrastructure. This proposal builds the AI Innovation Engine — a platform where faculty post real-world AI challenges, student teams build solutions using shared open-source tools, and every completed project deposits a reusable skill into a compound library that makes the next project faster. The Engine is not new infrastructure — it is a coordination layer connecting existing Gies assets: Sachdev's Canvas MCP (90+ AI tools, adopted in 6 courses), DSRS's compute cluster (486 CPU cores, A100 GPUs, self-hosted LLMs), and the Magelli Office's 200+ annual client projects. Zhang contributes the first finance-domain AI challenges and AI decision-making research methodology. As a natural byproduct, the Engine captures IRB-consented interaction data — targeting 10,000+ annotated student-AI interactions in Year 1 — available to any Gies faculty member studying AI adoption, learning analytics, or decision-making. An April 2026 buildathon piloting the cross-departmental collaboration is already underway. Expected outputs: 3-4 publications, an open research dataset, and preliminary data for an NSF Cyberlearning proposal ($500K-$750K).
