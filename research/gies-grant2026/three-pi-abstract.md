# Abstract

**Title:** Studying Human-AI Collaboration in Business Education: A Cross-Departmental Research Platform Built on Existing Gies Infrastructure

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Business Administration
**Co-Is:** Qingquan Zhang, Clinical Assistant Professor, Finance; Matias Carrasco Kind, Research Assistant Professor, Accountancy / Director, DSRS

**Funding Request:** Medium ($75,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Field study (longitudinal), quasi-experimental, archival

---

Generative AI is transforming business practice, yet we lack empirical infrastructure to study how students develop AI collaboration skills in realistic settings. This proposal builds a cross-departmental research platform that instruments AI-assisted student projects across Business Administration, Finance, and Accountancy, capturing fine-grained interaction data for studying human-AI collaborative decision-making. The platform leverages two major existing Gies assets: Sachdev's Canvas MCP (90+ open-source AI tools, adopted in 6 courses) and DSRS's computational infrastructure (486 CPU cores, A100 GPUs, Atlas agentic framework). Zhang contributes domain-specific AI decision tasks in finance and alternative data. Year 1 pilots instrumented data collection across 3 courses (~300 students), producing a curated dataset of 10,000+ annotated interactions. Year 2 scales to 8 courses across departments and publishes the open dataset for any Gies researcher. Expected outputs: 3-4 co-authored publications targeting *MIS Quarterly*, *Management Science*, and domain venues, plus preliminary data for an NSF Cyberlearning proposal. The platform serves as shared research infrastructure that any Gies faculty member can use to study AI-related phenomena, multiplying the return on a single investment.
