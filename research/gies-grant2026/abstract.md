# Abstract

**Title:** Building Research Infrastructure for Studying Human-AI Collaboration in Business Education at Scale

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Department of Business Administration

**Funding Request:** Large ($150,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Field study, with complementary qualitative and computational analyses

---

Business schools worldwide are integrating AI into curricula, yet we lack empirical infrastructure to study how students learn to collaborate with AI systems in realistic business contexts. This proposal funds the development of a shared research platform — the AI Innovation Engine — that captures fine-grained, IRB-consented interaction data as students build AI solutions for real challenges posted by faculty, staff, and corporate partners. The platform generates three scholarly contributions: (1) a longitudinal field study of human-AI collaborative skill development across 500+ students and 60+ projects in Year 1, producing an annotated corpus of 15,000+ task-level interactions; (2) empirical grounding for emerging theories of human-AI collective intelligence and organizational attention to AI; and (3) an open research dataset enabling Gies faculty across departments to study AI-mediated decision-making, learning analytics, and technology adoption. The infrastructure builds on existing Gies assets (Magelli Office projects, Canvas MCP open-source tools, student builder organizations) and is designed to serve multiple investigators over many years, with a target of 3-5 peer-reviewed publications and one external grant application (NSF) within the funding period.
