# Proposal Idea 2: AI Tools and Learning Outcomes — A Natural Experiment Across Six Courses

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Department of Business Administration

**Tier:** Small ($20,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Field study (natural experiment), archival

---

## Elevator Pitch

Canvas MCP — an open-source AI toolkit with 90+ tools — is already deployed in 6 Gies courses and not deployed in dozens of comparable sections. This creates a natural experiment. For $20K, we hire an RA and buy data analysis tools to measure whether AI-augmented course infrastructure changes student learning outcomes, engagement patterns, and assignment quality. No new platform to build — just rigorous analysis of what's already happening.

---

## Abstract (~180 words)

AI tools are rapidly entering business education, yet we lack causal evidence on whether AI-augmented course infrastructure improves student learning outcomes. This proposal exploits a natural experiment at Gies College of Business: six courses currently use Canvas MCP, an open-source AI toolkit with 90+ tools for assignment feedback, peer review management, and course analytics, while comparable sections of the same or similar courses do not. We propose a quasi-experimental study comparing learning outcomes (assignment grades, course completion, student satisfaction), engagement patterns (LMS activity logs, assignment submission timing), and assignment quality (rubric scores, revision rates) across AI-augmented and non-augmented course sections over two semesters (Fall 2026, Spring 2027). The study controls for instructor effects, course level, and student demographics using propensity score matching. This design requires no new infrastructure — only systematic data extraction from Canvas LMS (with IRB approval) and statistical analysis. Expected outputs include 1-2 peer-reviewed publications on AI in business education and a reusable methodology for evaluating AI tools across any LMS-based institution.

---

## Research Questions

1. Do students in AI-augmented courses (Canvas MCP) demonstrate measurably different learning outcomes compared to students in comparable non-augmented sections?
2. How does AI-augmented course infrastructure affect student engagement patterns (submission timing, LMS activity, help-seeking behavior)?
3. What instructor adoption patterns predict effective vs. ineffective use of AI course tools?

- **External funding trajectory:** Pilot results position an NSF IUSE proposal (typical award $300K-$500K).
- **Strategic priority:** AI in education is a top College priority. This provides evidence to guide the strategy.

## Weaknesses to Address

- Natural experiment means no random assignment — need strong matching strategy
- Canvas MCP varies in how instructors use it — need to account for dosage/fidelity
- As sole PI without tenure-track co-investigator, may need to strengthen methods section with an Education or IS collaborator
