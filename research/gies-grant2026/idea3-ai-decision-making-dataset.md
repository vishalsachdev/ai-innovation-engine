# Proposal Idea 3: A Shared Experimental Dataset of AI-Assisted Business Decision-Making

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Department of Business Administration

**Tier:** Medium ($50,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Experimental (Gies Business Research Lab)

---

## Elevator Pitch

Faculty across Accountancy, Finance, Marketing, and Strategy are all asking the same question: "How does AI change decision-making in my domain?" But each runs isolated experiments with incompatible designs. This proposal creates a shared experimental platform in the Gies Business Research Lab — standardized AI-assisted and unassisted conditions across 4 business domains — producing a multi-domain dataset that 10+ Gies researchers can publish from. One investment, many papers.

---

## Abstract (~195 words)

Generative AI is transforming business decision-making across every functional domain, yet empirical research remains fragmented: individual faculty design one-off experiments using incompatible tasks, AI interfaces, and measurement instruments. This proposal builds a shared experimental infrastructure within the Gies Business Research Lab that standardizes the study of AI-assisted decision-making across four business domains: financial analysis, marketing strategy, operational planning, and ethical reasoning. We develop a common experimental protocol — participants complete realistic business tasks with and without AI assistance, with standardized measures of decision quality, confidence calibration, process efficiency, and appropriate reliance — that individual investigators can extend with domain-specific hypotheses. The infrastructure includes: (a) a configurable AI assistant interface for experimental conditions (varying AI capability, explanation style, and autonomy level); (b) a battery of validated business decision tasks across four domains; and (c) a shared dataset architecture enabling cross-domain comparison. Year 1 runs a pilot study (N=200) across two domains; Year 2 scales to all four domains (N=600) with domain-expert co-investigators. Expected output: 4-6 co-authored publications and an open dataset serving Gies researchers for 3+ years.

---

## Research Questions

1. How does AI assistance affect the quality, speed, and confidence calibration of business decisions across functional domains?
2. Do patterns of appropriate vs. inappropriate AI reliance vary systematically by domain characteristics (structured vs. unstructured, quantitative vs. qualitative)?
3. What individual differences (domain expertise, AI experience, cognitive style) predict effective AI-assisted decision-making?

## Why This Fits the CFP

- **"Acquisition costs of human-subjects data"** and **"Fees for acquisition of human participants from the Gies Business Research Lab"** — directly listed as eligible uses
- **Multi-investigator by design:** The entire point is a shared dataset across departments. Each domain expert co-authors papers from their domain while the PI publishes cross-domain findings.
- **Hits multiple evaluation criteria simultaneously:**
  - "High potential to contribute to multiple projects across multiple researchers" — 4+ research teams from a single investment
  - "Lead to material external sources of additional funding" — multi-domain pilot data is exactly what NSF BCS (Behavioral and Cognitive Sciences) and DARPA programs want as preliminary results
  - "Advance the College's current strategic priorities" — AI is the top strategic priority
- **Builds Gies's lab capabilities** — the CFP explicitly lists "improvements to the capabilities of any current research initiatives or laboratories"

## Strengths

- Directly uses the Gies Business Research Lab — the CFP mentions this explicitly
- Multi-department by design — strongest possible signal on "multiple researchers" criterion
- Clean experimental methodology that reviewers trust
- Practical outputs: every domain expert gets a paper, the Lab gets a new capability
- Cross-domain comparison is genuinely novel — most AI decision-making studies are single-domain

## Weaknesses to Address

- Requires buy-in from domain experts (mitigated: they get a paper with minimal effort — PI builds the infrastructure, they contribute domain tasks and co-author)
- PI is clinical faculty, not an experimental methodologist — may want a co-PI from Psychology or quantitative methods
- $50K is modest for 800 participants — may need to argue that Business Research Lab fees at Gies are below external Prolific/MTurk rates
