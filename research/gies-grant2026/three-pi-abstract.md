# Abstract

**Title:** The Gies AI Innovation Engine: Shared Research Infrastructure for AI-Driven Business Education

**PI:** Vishal Sachdev, Clinical Associate Professor, Business Administration
**Co-Is:** Qingquan Zhang, Adjunct Clinical Assistant Professor, Finance; Matias Carrasco Kind, Research Assistant Professor, Accountancy / Director, DSRS

**Funding Request:** Medium ($75,000)

**Primary Methodology:** Field study, archival, experimental

**Attachments:** CVs for PI and Co-Is; prior funding progress update

---

Every semester, thousands of Gies students use AI tools across business courses, yet this activity produces no shared research data and no reusable infrastructure. This proposal builds the AI Innovation Engine — a platform where faculty post real-world AI challenges, student teams build solutions using shared open-source tools, and every completed project deposits a reusable skill into a compound library that makes the next project faster. The Engine is a coordination layer connecting existing Gies assets: Sachdev's Canvas MCP (90+ AI tools, adopted in 6 courses), DSRS's compute cluster and iKompete challenge platform (Carrasco Kind, peer-reviewed in Gateways 2023), and the Magelli Office's 200+ annual client projects. Zhang contributes finance-domain AI challenges and decision-making research methodology. As a byproduct, the Engine captures fine-grained interaction data — targeting 10,000+ logged student-AI interactions in Year 1. The instrumentation layer is designed so that additional Gies faculty can integrate their courses and collect data under their own IRB protocols. A buildathon scheduled for April 2026 is the first pilot of this three-PI collaboration. Expected outputs: 2-4 publications, a compound skill library, and preliminary data for an NSF Cyberlearning proposal.
